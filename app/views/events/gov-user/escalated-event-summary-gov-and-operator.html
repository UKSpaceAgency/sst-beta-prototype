Report number 1, updated on 01/11/2023 09:00:00

Escalated report Escalated

Executive summary

The UK Space Agency Orbital Analyst team are monitoring a potential collision, which is summarised in the table below. This is a low probability of collision (PoC), with Starlink likely to manoeuvre to reduce the PoC. If the event were to occur it could potentially result in a full breakup of both objects, with some UK licensed satellites seeing an increased risk of potential collision events due to the fragments produced.

Next steps

With a manoeuvre expected to take place, there is unlikely to be another update prior to the event due to the lower risk; an email will be generated post event. For Low Risk events, updates are only sent if the probability breaches a Medium Risk threshold, even if the probability has increased. A close-down report will be sent in all cases. For Medium/High-risk events, updates are sent as soon as new information is available.

Potential impact of event

Immediate impact

Should the collision occur, it would likely result in a full breakup of both objects. In this worst-case scenario, an estimation of the number of fragments greater than 10cm is 223.

Short term impact

Should the collision occur, the debris would generate an increased risk to satellites in LEO. We are predicting that the event would result in an 8.7% increase in potential collisions across all UK satellites in LEO.

Long term impact

The collision would occur at an altitude of approximately 1200 km. This is the operational altitude of OneWeb and a significant proportion of debris here would likely have an orbital lifetime exceeding 100 years. The risk would therefore be sustained for significant periods of time and could eventually impact a broad range of satellites and missions including human spaceflight.
