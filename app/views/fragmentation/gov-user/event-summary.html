Executive summary

The UK Space Agency is actively monitoring a non-UK fragmentation event involving RESURS P1 (NORAD 60374). Current tracking shows approximately 88 pieces of debris associated with this fragmentation, though work continues to identify and catalogue other potential debris objects. This number could therefore change significantly as more data is received.

This is a medium risk event due to the number of fragments produced, with some UK licensed satellites, as well as Human Spaceflight Missions, seeing an increased risk of potential collision. The UK Space Agency is tracking the upcoming re-entry of an object from space into the Earth’s atmosphere, posing a potential threat to life and property on the ground. All re-entered debris should be considered as hazardous until proven otherwise.

Potential impact of event

This incident has been intially assess as MEDIUM RISK due to the number of reported fragments released

Short term

The fragment tally is currently assessed to be >300 pieces of debris, released at approximately 350km. As more information is received, the number of fragments could change significantly.

Our collision assessments show a 1.1% increase in potential collisions involving UK satellites in LEO. Conjunction screening will take place once data is catalogued and will be distributed as per normal via Monitor Space Hazards.

Long term

At this altitude, the majority of debris will decay quickly. Therefore, the debris cloud poses a low, long-term risk to satellites and manned spacecraft. Understanding the extent of the debris generated will take weeks and the full extent will never be understood as fragments below 10cm will not be able to be tracked and catalogued.

Guidance on response

UK response

Sovereign sensors have been tasked to provide data on the fragments. Observations have been received; more will be generated over the weekend.
