What are these ratings?

Information used to generate these outputs:

  • Probability of collision: generated based on orbit determination calculations. High represents a probability over 1%; medium over 0.1%; and low below 0.1%.
  • Potential impact: generated based on potential risk to UK assets, other sectors, loss of services, reputational damage and financial loss.

What do the ratings mean?

  • High: requires urgent action.
  • Medium: monitor closely and notify internal team.
  • Low: monitor over time.

What this information means

  • ERF levels, Emergency Response Framework levels, are explained in our ERF information page. To summarise,...

  • Probability of collision above x% is usually a cause for concern and the event should be monitored. For any probability over x%, the UKSA recommends escalating the event and prompting an avoidance manoeuvre, if possible.

  • Risk to UK assets reflects whether there is an immediate risk to UK spacecraft if this collision occurs.

Data sources and reliability

  • Data is originally sourced from the US database, Spacetrack. We have generated a more realistic probability of collision using our own more refined estimating technique. This means that our data outputs are usually more realistic than those generated by Spacetrack.

  • Data is received from Spacetrack every 8 hours or so, therefore, you can expect updates once or twice per day.

  • Where possible, we may also task UK radars and sensors to give us rapid tracking data on the objects involved.
