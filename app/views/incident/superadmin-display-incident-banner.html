Manage incident banner

In the case of an incident, the Monitor your satellites service will remain live, but it is important to notify all Monitor your satellites users.

Superadmins are able to select a banner that will be visible at the top of each page, warning users that the information presented on Monitor your satellites may not be accurate.

  1. 1. Select the incident banner you would like to display

    Important

    Due to an ongoing incident, Monitor your satellites may display inaccurate information at this time.

    Contact monitoryoursatellites@ukspaceagency.gov.uk for more information

    Important

    Due to a technical issue ingesting conjunction data messages, Monitor your satellites may display inaccurate information at this time.

    You can visit the external data sources performance page to see when the last fetch of data was.
    Contact monitoryoursatellites@ukspaceagency.gov.uk for more information.

    Important

    Due to a technical issue ingesting object data, Monitor your satellites may display inaccurate information at this time.

    You can visit the external data sources performance page to see when the last fetch of data was.
    Contact monitoryoursatellites@ukspaceagency.gov.uk for more information.

    Important

    Due to a technical issue ingesting ingesting orbital analyses, Monitor your satellites may display inaccurate information at this time.

    You can visit the external data sources performance page to see when the last fetch of data was.
    Contact monitoryoursatellites@ukspaceagency.gov.uk for more information.

  2. 2. Choose time period to display banner for

    The incident banner will be visible on the site until you manually remove it.

    Enter start date
    For example, 12 11 2007
    Enter start time
    For example, 08:10
    Enter end date
    For example, 12 11 2007
    Enter end time
    For example, 16:10
