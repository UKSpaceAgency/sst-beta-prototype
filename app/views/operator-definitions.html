Event parameters

Primary and secondary objects: The primary and secondary object distinction is taken from Space Track conjunction data messages (CDMs). This means that the primary object will not always be your own satellite.

Data sources

The Consulatative Commmittee for Space Data Systems describes the miss distance as "the norm of the relative position vector. It indicates how close the two objects are at the time of the predicted encounter."

Monitor my satellites uses the RTN coordinate framework to describe miss distances.

R (Radial) is the unit vector in the radial direction pointed outward from the center of the central body.

T (Transverse) is the unit vector perpendicular to the R vector in the direction of the spacecraft velocity.

N (Normal) is the unit vector normal to the satellite’s inertial orbit plane (in the direction of the satellite’s angular momentum) that completes the right-hand coordinate frame.

In-track miss distance refers to the transverse miss distance.

Cross-track miss distance refers to the normal miss distance.
